What is a simulation?

A simulation is a program that imitates a real-world process. It uses a model, accepts inputs, follows rules, and produces results.

🎯

Why do programmers use them?

Simulations are useful when real testing would be expensive, dangerous, slow, or impossible to repeat many times.

🌦️

What affects accuracy?

A simulation is only as good as its model. Missing variables, weak assumptions, or unrealistic data can make results less reliable.

📈

What should we observe?

Look for patterns over many trials. One result can be random, but many results can reveal a trend.
