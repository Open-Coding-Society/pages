## About This Analysis

This infographic summarizes a comprehensive UX analysis of [Poway Veterans Organization](https://powayveterans.org/) — a 100% volunteer-run nonprofit serving veterans and their dependents in Poway, Ramona, and surrounding San Diego communities since 2014.

### The Core Finding

PVO does not mainly need a flashy new feature. It needs a clearer **help pathway** — a simple, trustworthy system that tells veterans, families, volunteers, and donors exactly where to go, what to do, and what happens next.

### The Strategic Direction

> Poway Veterans Organization should redesign its website from an information site into a **guided local support portal** for veterans, families, volunteers, and donors.

---

## What PVO Does Well

- Strong local mission clearly communicated
- 95%+ of funds go directly to veterans
- Fully volunteer-run — a powerful trust signal
- Multiple actions already supported: apply, donate, volunteer, events, scholarships
- Past services page provides real proof of impact

---

## The 7-Phase Roadmap

| Phase | Focus | Priority |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 1 | **Get Help Hub** — "Need Help? Start Here" | 🔴 Highest |
| 2 | **Homepage Restructure** — organize by intent | 🔵 High |
| 3 | **Local Resource Map** — VA, housing, food, legal, transport | 🔵 High |
| 4 | **Volunteer Redesign** — roles, time commitment, examples | 🟡 Medium |
| 5 | **Impact Page** — scannable summary of help provided | 🟡 Medium |
| 6 | **Freshness Updates** — events, blog, newsletter | 🟡 Medium |
| 7 | **Form Simplification** — lighter, more accessible forms | ⚪ Lower |

---

## The Missing Thing (In Plain Language)

For a railroad group, the key missing feature is a schedule and live map.  
For PVO, the missing thing is:

**A clear veteran-support pathway with a local resource map and a much easier way to understand help, apply, volunteer, and trust the organization.**
