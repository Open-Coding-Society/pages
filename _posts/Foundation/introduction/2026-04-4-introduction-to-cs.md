## What is Computer Science?
Computer Science, if we were to describe it in a sentence, is essentially using computers **to solve** problems. Computers are our powerful tools in this subject in which we can solve all sorts of problems.

Take a look at the diagram below which illustrates this very concept (courtesy to CS50 for the diagram):

<img src="{{site.baseurl}}/images/input-to-output-diagram.png" height="100%">

In computer science we're constantly taking in input (finding problems) and turning them into output (the problems solved) and in between is the secret sauce (the code) that turns the input into the output.

If you can think of computer science as fundamentally the diagram above, you're in a great spot.

## How to Approach This Class
This class can be described as a "trial by fire." What that means is you may feel overwhelmed, confused, and out of place. If you do feel like this or end up so, don't worry. That is part of the process, especially in the first few weeks of this class. Depending on your background, computer science might be a completely novel thing to you. And so, you'll be learning a lot of new things really fast, but it's nothing you can't do.

If you are new, this course is just for you. And I'd recommend you continue going through the lessons in Sprint 1 (what we call the first few weeks of this class) and you'll continue to build your foundations there.
